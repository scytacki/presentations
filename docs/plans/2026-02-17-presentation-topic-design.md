# Presentation Design: AI Is Changing How We Work Together

## Unifying Topic

"AI is changing how we work together — here's what that looks like"

## Audience

[Education Technology Research Group](../../audiences/ed-tech-research-group.md) — primarily PIs, PMs, and researchers. Everyone has some experience with AI. The group did "build something with AI" retreats over a year ago, so the novelty of AI-assisted building is past.

## Presentation Stance

- Not prescriptive — grounded in real experience, not advice
- ~15 minutes, leaving room for discussion
- Followed by a second presentation showing a developer/PI/visual designer collaboration over 4 hours

## Through-line

The surprising part of building a simulation over two weeks with AI wasn't what got built — it was how the way of working changed. AI made it worthwhile to create rich planning documents that served double duty: guiding AI code generation and giving PIs/PMs real visibility into the work.

## Structure

### 1. Opening — "What would you build?"

Brief callback to the group's AI retreats from a year ago. Acknowledge everyone has tried building with AI. But a lot has changed since then. Two weeks ago, I started building a simulation — here's what happened.

### 2. The work — how AI changed the process

Walk through building the ocean simulation:

- Started by creating planning documents with AI: a science document, a roadmap, detailed phase plans
- These documents would have taken days to create manually — and wouldn't have been worth maintaining
- With AI, they were cheap to create *and* directly useful: they guided AI code generation and gave PIs/PMs real visibility
- Shared updated documents and intermediate simulation builds along the way
- PIs/PMs read the docs and played with the simulation versions

Key point: AI made a way of working practical that wouldn't have been worth the effort before. Documents that serve double duty — collaboration with AI and collaboration with people.

### 3. What this opens up

Broaden from personal experience to the group:

- PIs/PMs could create their own prototypes to explore ideas before writing proposals or kicking off development (ties into the second presentation)
- Researchers could build custom tools for their own data analysis needs
- Collaboration touchpoints change — instead of waiting for a developer to build something, people can engage with evolving documents and working prototypes

Note: not all development work fits the simulation pattern. Adding a feature to an existing platform doesn't have a science doc. But there may still be meaningful design documents that identify edge cases and decisions — the underlying pattern of AI making richer planning documents worthwhile still applies.

This section connects experience to possibilities relevant to each role. Real examples where they exist, open questions where they don't.

### 4. Open questions for discussion

End with genuine questions, not conclusions:

- How do we give feedback on documents and prototypes that are moving faster than our usual review cycles?
- What kinds of planning documents would be most useful for your role?
- How does this change what we include in proposals or project plans?

Keep short — seed the discussion, don't answer the questions. Bridges naturally into the second presentation.
